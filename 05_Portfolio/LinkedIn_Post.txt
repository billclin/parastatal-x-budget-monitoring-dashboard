🏛️ I just built a full financial analytics solution for a fictional Kenyan parastatal — from raw data to a Power BI dashboard in one end-to-end project.

Here's the management problem I set out to solve 👇

Most public sector finance teams are flying blind. By the time a monthly report reaches management, the data is stale, the exceptions are buried in spreadsheets, and the year-end forecast exists only in someone's head.

I wanted to show what a modern, data-driven approach looks like in a Kenyan public sector context.

---

THE PROJECT: Parastatal X Dynamic Budget Monitoring, Forecasting and Management Dashboard

✅ 13 synthetic datasets | 18,896+ rows of realistic data
✅ 32 cost centres | 13 directorates | 8 regional offices
✅ 3 financial years (FY2023/24 to FY2025/26)
✅ Python data generation (reproducible, seed=42)
✅ Star schema data model (12 fact tables, 14 dimensions)
✅ 70+ DAX measures
✅ 500 lines of Power Query M code
✅ 4-method financial forecast model
✅ 12-page Power BI dashboard design
✅ Management commentary and action plan

---

THE KENYAN CONTEXT I EMBEDDED:

The data reflects how Kenyan parastatals actually operate — financial years running July to June, Authority to Incur Expenditure workflow, supplementary budgets, vote code controls, AIA revenue streams, donor-funded programmes, quarterly M&E reporting, and audit readiness requirements.

This isn't generic finance analytics. It's designed for the public sector environment.
